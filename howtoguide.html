How to Survive a Dinosaur Attack

A kid-friendly jungle guide for brave explorers

Quick steps to stay safe

Pretend-play scenario: If a dinosaur shows up in your imagination jungle, here’s how to stay cool, think smart, and make great choices. This guide is for fun learning—be brave, be kind, and be clever!

  • Pause and breathe like a calm explorer.
    Take one deep breath in, one slow breath out. Quiet minds make quick, smart choices.
  • Look and listen for clues.
    Big footsteps mean a heavier dino; rustling bushes mean it might be small and speedy. Stay alert and curious.
  • Move to safe, sturdy places.
    Imagine stepping behind a strong tree, a big rock, or into a cozy cave. Pick quiet paths and avoid open fields.
  • Be still if it hasn’t noticed you.
    Sometimes the smartest move is a freeze. Slow, small movements help you stay hidden like a jungle shadow.
  • If it’s near, use gentle distractions.
    Drop a hat or backpack to the side and quietly step the other way. Keep your path calm and steady.
  • Choose smart paths: up, around, or away.
    Walk where it’s harder for big feet to go—over roots, around rocks, and along twisty trails. No running, just smooth sneaking.
  • Team up and signal softly.
    Stay with your explorer group. Use quiet hand signals and calm whispers. Everyone moves together.
  • Know your dinos, know your plan.
    Big plant-eaters might ignore you; small fast ones are curious. Learn and adapt like a scientist.
  • Celebrate safe choices.
    Once you’re clear, high-five your team, sip some water, and write down what worked for next time.
Explorer tip: Quiet feet, steady steps, and clever paths beat speed. Patience is your jungle superpower.
